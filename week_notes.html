Week 5#

Tuesday#

  • Thermodyamics – important points

    • Equations 39 and 41 for the moist and liquid static energies:

      \[\begin{split} \begin{align} s_v &= c_p T + l_v r_v + gz \\ s_l & = c_p T - l_v r_l + gz \end{align} \end{split}\]

    • Equation 54 and 55 for the equivalent potential temperature and liquid water potential temperature

      \[\begin{split} \begin{align} \theta_e &= \theta \exp \left ( \frac{l_v r_{sat}}{c_p T} \right )\\ \theta_l &= \theta \exp \left ( - \frac{l_v r_l}{c_p T} \right ) \end{align} \end{split}\]

    • Note that \(s_v\), \(s_l\), \(\theta_v\) and \(\theta_l\) all approximately label the same moist adiabat on a tephigram, because they are all approximately conserved for adiabatic ascent and descent

Week 7#

Tuesday#

  • Go over the material from Stull Chapter 7 page 267-271 on the Businger-Dyer relationships, which is repeated in Chapter 9 p. 383-385.

  • Paper: Surface-layer scaling at Cabauw: Verkaik and Holtslag

    • Take home point from that paper: Surface layer scaling can be disturbed by upstream changes in surface roughness (although it still does pretty well)

    • Figure 5 on page 710 shows the agreement/disagreement with Businger-Dyer relationships depending on upstream conditions/wind direction
